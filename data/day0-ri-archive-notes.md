## Search Directions Not Adopted / Skipped

- **Library of Congress:** loc.gov and blogs.loc.gov returned 403 responses to every automated request from this tool, preventing item-by-item verification of the original copyright wording. LOC items were therefore not included in the formal seed archive in this round. Search results confirmed that many relevant items do exist at LOC (Bain News Service negatives, the 1876 print “Prisoners returning from work,” and others). The developer should verify them through a browser while signed in, then add them using the same table format as this note, with `source: "Library of Congress"`.
- **Close-up stone / gneiss textures:** No institution-specific close-up photographs of quarry or wall stone were found (the Wikimedia result for “Robben Island” was a false match caused by the shared word “Island”). #15 (HABS east-side view of the Octagon) and #14 (HABS lighthouse photograph) show stone walls, but neither is a close-up texture. **During the Phase 0.5 island visit, prioritize taking a close-up texture photograph of the lighthouse or seawall**, then use it directly as a texture asset; this is more efficient than continuing the search.
- **1969 Philip Johnson / Burgee master plan:** The plan's existence was confirmed (the Metropolitan Museum of Art's 1969 exhibition “The Island Nobody Knows”), but no public-domain scan of Metropolitan Museum or UDC archive material was found on Wikimedia / NYPL. Its copyright status is unclear, so it was not adopted, avoiding a fabricated URL.
- **1920s–1940s Welfare Island maps:** Multiple searches found nothing. Existing maps cluster around 1838 (bridge engineering drawing, also map-like) and 1886 (coastal survey). The “renamed Welfare Island” portion of the time corridor should use the date range of photographs / prints rather than depend on another map.
